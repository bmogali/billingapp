S-Lift/Feather Lift Consent Form

  1. I, hereby consent that Dr. Ayman El-Attar, or his assistants or his designees, to perform Feather face lift surgery on myself. The procedure has been explained to me by the doctor and/or his staff and I completely understand the nature and consequences of the surgery. The following points have been specifically made clear:

    • That medicine is not an exact science and complications such as death, although extremely rare, may occur.
    • That swelling, bruising and mild discomfort usually occurs.
    • That no guarantees with respect to the final outcome and its longevity can be offered.
    • That infection is possible.
    • That sensation may be altered or completely lost.
    • That function may be altered and that rarely injuries to the facial motor nerves can occur, resulting in weakness of facial muscles.
    • That delayed wound healing and/or poor scarring may occur.
    • That revisions may be necessary.
    • That the healing process takes time and the final result will not be readily visible for many weeks and possibly months.
    • That bleeding may occur and should blood collect (a hematoma), this may require further surgical treatment.
    • That skin loss may occur and that smoking may cause this problem.
    • That chronic or persistent problems may occur which require treatment.
    • That asymmetry (one side of the face does not match the other side) is possible.
    • That small areas of temporary or permanent hair loss may occur.

  2. I understand that a “S” Lift or a Feather Lift is essentially a non-invasive face lift and not a neck lift. As such the improvement of the neck is limited.

  3. I recognize that, during the course of the operation, unforeseen conditions may necessitate additional or different procedures than those set forth above. I therefore further authorize and request that the above-named doctor, his assistants or his designees perform such procedures as are, in his professional judgment, necessary and desirable, including, but not limited to, procedures involving pathology and radiology. The authority granted under this Paragraph 3 shall extend to remedying conditions that are not known to the above doctors at the time the operation is commenced.

  4. I understand that transverse forehead lines, deeps glabellar creases and transverse lines at the root of nose can be improved by brow lifting but these lines cannot be eliminated. Malar pouches below the eyelid bags do not disappear after face lifting and occasionally are accentuated during the immediate post operative period. Nasolabial folds can be softened but they can never be eliminated.

  5. “Marionette lines” from the corner of the mouth to the border of the jaw never disappear completely.

  6. A short neck precludes an ideal cervicomental angle. (i.e.) neck lift.

  7. “Ptotic” or hanging salivary glands are at angle of your jaws are difficult to deal with.

  8. Skin has numerous fine wrinkles and they may need a chemical peel or laser.

  9. Smoking increases the risk of skin slough. “Rees and Colleagues” have reported a risk of skin slough 12 times greater than non-smokers. Patients are requested to stop smoking for two weeks. However the risk in smokers remain.

  10. I am aware that the practice of medicine and surgery is not an exact science, and I acknowledge that no guarantees have been made to me as to the results of the operation or procedure. In the absence of any purposeful intent to harm I will not sue.
